I am a PhD student in the Statistics department at Columbia University, advised by [David M. Blei](http://www.cs.columbia.edu/~blei/). Previously, I completed B.A.s in Computer Science and Statistics at UC Berkeley, where I worked with [Jasjeet Sekhon](https://statistics.yale.edu/people/jas-sekhon) and [Chunlei Liu](https://www2.eecs.berkeley.edu/Faculty/Homepages/chunleiliu.html).

My research interests span **machine learning** with applications to interpretability (mechanistic interpretability, tree-based models, sparsity), causal inference (causal representation learning, invariance), and neuroscience; **sequential decision making** (adaptive experimentation, Bayesian optimization, reinforcement learning); and **generative modeling** (diffusion models, test-time scaling and steering, variational learning).
